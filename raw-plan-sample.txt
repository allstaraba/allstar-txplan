### Communication

Brandon demonstrates pervasive and significant deficits in functional communication skills across all verbal operant domains. Per observation on 04/06/2026, Brandon's vocal repertoire is severely limited and characterized primarily by scripting and echolalic vocalizations. He is able to emit contextually appropriate vocalizations including "dada" when referring to his father and "grandma" when referring to his maternal grandmother, suggesting emerging speaker-listener repertoires with familiar individuals in his immediate environment. Brandon demonstrates the ability to spontaneously vocalize short words and phrases from highly preferred songs when motivated by auditory stimuli, indicating that his echoic repertoire is more developed when embedded within preferred musical contexts than when prompted during structured assessment conditions. He was observed to emit the phrase "let's go" in a contextually appropriate manner during a transitional moment, suggesting the presence of an emerging mand repertoire for activities.

However, when systematically probed during structured assessment conditions, Brandon was unable to emit his name or age in response to intraverbal prompts (e.g., "What's your name?" "How old are you?"). He does not demonstrate the ability to mand for basic needs, including requesting food, drink, preferred items, assistance, breaks, or cessation of aversive stimuli using any augmented or unaided communication modality. Brandon is unable to tact common objects, pictures, or environmental stimuli when presented with exemplars during structured probing. He does not demonstrate receptive identification skills when given instructions to select items from a field (e.g., "touch the [item]"). Brandon demonstrates significant deficits in listener responding repertoires, as he was unable to follow one-step motor instructions such as "clap hands," "touch nose," or "wave" during structured assessment. These deficits in foundational verbal behavior repertoires are consistent with his VB-MAPP total score of 6.0 out of 170, placing his overall performance within Level 1 milestones (0-18 months developmental equivalent).

Per caregiver report, Brandon occasionally uses hand leading and physical manipulation of adults' hands to initiate requests for preferred items and activities. This behavior suggests that he has developed rudimentary pre-verbal requesting strategies but has not acquired the vocal, gestural, or augmented manding repertoires necessary for effective functional communication. The absence of a reliable manding repertoire is a significant contributor to the frequency and intensity of his challenging behaviors, as he is unable to appropriately communicate his needs, desires, or distress.

## Criterion-Referenced Assessment

### Verbal Behavior Milestones Assessment and Placement Program (VB-MAPP)

The Verbal Behavior Milestones Assessment and Placement Program (VB-MAPP) is a criterion-referenced tool, curriculum guide and skill tracking system that assesses milestones in various social-communication and early academic areas as they relate to typically developing peers. The VB-MAPP is based on B.F. Skinner's (1957) analysis of verbal behavior, established developmental milestones, and research from the field of behavior analysis. The assessment establishes three developmental levels based on typical age of acquisition: Level 1 (0-18 months), Level 2 (18-30 months), and Level 3 (30-48 months).

The VB-MAPP Milestones Assessment was administered on 04/06/2026 by Sajeeta Dhungana, BCBA. Brandon achieved a total Milestones score of **6.0 out of 170 possible points**, placing his performance entirely within Level 1 milestones (0-18 month developmental equivalent). This score represents a significant discrepancy from his chronological age of 7 years 0 months and indicates severe delays across all assessed verbal behavior and related skill domains.

**Level 1 Performance (0-18 months):** Brandon's scores were distributed across Level 1 domains with partial credit obtained in select areas. He demonstrated emerging skills in the following domains:

- **Mand:** Brandon demonstrated the ability to use hand leading and physical manipulation to communicate wants, and emits limited contextually appropriate vocalizations (e.g., "dada," "grandma," "let's go") that function as mands within familiar contexts. He does not yet demonstrate a generalized mand repertoire across multiple items, actions, or communicative partners.

- **Tact:** Brandon demonstrated minimal tacting abilities limited to naming two familiar people. He does not tact common objects, pictures, actions, or environmental stimuli.

- **Listener Responding:** Brandon demonstrated minimal listener responding skills. He did not follow one-step motor instructions or receptively identify items from a field during structured assessment probes.

- **Visual Perceptual/Matching-to-Sample (VP/MTS):** Brandon demonstrated the ability to complete simple matching tasks with cause-and-effect toys (e.g., shape sorters), suggesting emerging visual perceptual discrimination skills.

- **Play:** Brandon demonstrated functional play restricted to cause-and-effect toys and emerging pretend play with figurines per caregiver report.

- **Social:** Brandon demonstrated minimal social skills with emerging responsiveness to familiar adults.

- **Imitation:** Brandon did not demonstrate motor imitation skills during structured assessment probes.
