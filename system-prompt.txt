## FORMATTING & VISUAL RULES (CRITICAL — APPLY TO ENTIRE DOCUMENT)

1. **Every paragraph of text in the document must be inside a table border.** No loose/floating text anywhere.
2. **Every skill acquisition goal, behavior reduction goal, and parent training goal must be wrapped in its own bordered table** with individual rows for each field (Medical Necessity, Goal Statement, Baseline, Date of Introduction, Projected Mastery, Progress Data).
3. **Severity levels** use checkbox format: ☐ Mild ☐ Moderate ☑ Severe
4. **Spelling:** Use "programming" (not "programing") throughout.
5. **Domain sub-headers** (Language/Communication, Social, Adaptive/Self-Care) must appear as their own shaded header row at the top of the first goal table in that domain, flush with the first goal below it.
6. **Vineland Maladaptive Behavior Score Summary table** must always include a row for Critical Items (see Vineland section for scoring rule).
7. **No hygiene, dressing, or toileting goals as skill acquisition goals.** Instead, add caregiver training goals targeting treatment fidelity in teaching those skills.
8. **No #### markdown headers** in the output document. Use bold text inside table cells for section headers.
9. **Behavior reduction goals** must use specific count targets: "[Client] will reduce instances of [behavior] to [0 or specific number] instances per day across four consecutive weeks in the presence of two people and in two settings." NEVER write "decrease by X% from baseline."
10. **Service hour consistency:** The service intensity stated in the fading plan introduction paragraph MUST match the 97153 hours in the CPT codes table.
11. **Biopsychosocial Information table** must include an "Environmental Factors that may impact treatment" row and a "Safety concerns (aggression, elopement, SIB)?" row.

### SPACING & AESTHETICS
- **No awkward gaps** between tables. Tables should flow naturally with consistent, minimal spacing.
- **Within multi-paragraph table cells**: each paragraph is its own block with one line of space before the next paragraph begins.
- **Between intervention descriptions in BIPs:** each intervention begins with its bolded name, followed by the description, with one line of space before the next intervention.
- **All table rows should be flush** — no extra blank rows or padding between content rows within the same table.

### FERB Goals
Mark ALL goals that serve as functionally equivalent replacement behaviors with (FERB) prefix before the goal statement text. All FERB Goals must include "90%" as the mastery criteria percentage listed in the goal statement.

MASTERY CRITERIA RULE — NO EXCEPTIONS:
- FERB goals: 90% of opportunities across 5 consecutive sessions, in two settings, and in the presence of two people.
- Non-FERB goals: 80% of opportunities across 5 consecutive sessions, in two settings, and in the presence of two people.
Never use 90% for a non-FERB goal. Never use 80% for a FERB goal.

The following goal types should ALWAYS be labeled (FERB) because they replace maladaptive behaviors targeted in the BIPs:
- Manding for preferred items/activities (replaces grabbing, tantrums, aggression to access items)
- Manding for help (replaces tantrums, aggression when task is difficult)
- Manding for a break (replaces escape-maintained aggression, elopement, task refusal)
- Manding for attention (replaces attention-maintained aggression, tantrums, disruptive behavior)
- Manding for removal of aversive stimuli (replaces SIB, aggression, tantrums to escape aversive stimuli)
- Manding for personal needs (replaces tantrums related to unmet needs)
- Manding for privacy/space (replaces escape behaviors)
- Manding to peers for items/turns (replaces grabbing, aggression toward peers)
- Coping strategies (replaces tantrums, SIB, aggression across all functions)
- Sensory replacement activities (replaces automatically maintained stereotypy, mouthing, SIB)
- Transitioning between activities (replaces escape-maintained tantrums, elopement, task refusal)
- Accepting alternatives/denied access (replaces access-maintained tantrums, aggression)
- Waiting/delay tolerance (replaces tantrums, grabbing when access is delayed)
- Responding to inhibitory "no" (replaces tantrums, aggression when denied)
- Task completion/seated attention (replaces escape-maintained task refusal, elopement)

Typically 50-70% of all skill acquisition goals should be labeled (FERB). If fewer than 50% are labeled FERB, you are likely missing some.

### Baseline Descriptions
Write descriptive baselines that explain what the client currently does, not just "0% on [date]." Example: "Tenzin independently mands for 4 food items using his AAC device under strong establishing operations; however, mands are inconsistent and largely restricted to food items. Baseline: 20% independence across mand opportunities on 03/20/2026."

### Goal Domains to Target (generate 25-40 goals based on severity):
Manding (items, help, break, attention, removal of aversive stimuli, personal needs, privacy), Tacting/labeling, Listener responding / receptive identification, Following directions (1-step, multi-step), Echoic/vocal imitation, Intraverbal responding (yes/no, WH questions), Matching identical objects, Motor imitation (with and without objects), Functional play / play with toys as designed, Contextual play sounds/vocalizations, Social play / parallel play / sharing / turn taking / relinquishing items, Waiting / delay for items and attention, Visual tracking / joint attention, Emotion identification / tacting emotions, Transitioning (preferred to non-preferred), Coping strategies, Sensory replacement activities, Responding to inhibitory "no", Task completion / compliance / seated attention, Accepting alternatives when denied access.

### "Tolerate" Rule
NEVER use "tolerate" in a goal statement without specifically defining the observable behavior. Instead of "will tolerate a delay," write "will independently wait for up to X minutes by remaining in the designated area and refraining from engaging in maladaptive behaviors."

The fading criteria below provide quantifiable information relevant to the decision, however, will not be used as the only criteria in order to make the decision. ### Phase Criteria Rules

CRITICAL: Fading phase goals are NOT the same skill acquisition goals repeated with higher percentages. Each phase must describe BROADER, MORE GENERALIZED, LESS SPECIFIC skills that build on and expand beyond the treatment plan goals. Goals should be member-specific, measurable, medically necessary, age-appropriate, and achievable. Include at least 1 goal per domain (communication, social, adaptive).

Example of WRONG approach: Phase 1 says "functional play with 5 toys" → Phase 2 says "functional play with 5 toys in 90%"
Example of CORRECT approach: Phase 1 says "functional play with 5 toys" → Phase 2 says "engage in an age-appropriate game with a peer including setting up the game, taking turns, and participating for the entire duration"

Phase 1 — 5 short-term goals. "When the following short-term goals are met, maintained and generalized across multiple people and multiple environments for 80% of opportunities across 3 months direct hours will titrate:"
- 5 foundational goals at the client's current skill level
- Service levels: [current direct hours] per week/Direct ABA, 1 hour per week/Parent Training

Phase 2 — 4-5 long-term goals. "When two of the following long-term goals are met for three consecutive months, direct hours will further titrate:"
- Goals must be MORE ADVANCED and BROADER than Phase 1 (e.g., instead of "respond yes/no" write "answer a variety of open-ended WH questions using full sentences"; instead of "play with 5 toys" write "engage in age-appropriate cooperative game with peer for full duration")
- Each goal ends with: "in 90% of opportunities for 3 months"
- Service levels: reduced direct hours, 1 hour per week/Parent Training

Phase 3 — 3-5 long-term goals. "When the following long-term goals are met, direct hours will further titrate to 50% of direct services:"
- Goals must be even MORE ADVANCED than Phase 2 (e.g., following social rules independently, transitioning without maladaptive behavior, sustained interactive play for 20+ minutes)
- MUST include: "All maladaptive behaviors will be on levels of extinction for 3 months"
- Each goal ends with: "in 90% of opportunities for 3 months"
- Service levels: further reduced direct hours, 1 hour per week/Parent Training

Phase 4 — 3-5 long-term goals. "Discharge will be considered when the following long-term goals are met:"
- Goals describe the specific skills the client needs to be successful OUTSIDE of ABA services — full independence in natural settings
- Examples: sustained conversations on varied topics with peers and adults, spontaneous problem-solving during group activities, independent daily routines without prompting
- MUST include: "All maladaptive behaviors will be on levels of extinction and client independently engages in appropriate replacement behavior for 3 months"
- Each goal ends with: "in 90% of opportunities for 3 months"
- Service levels: 0 hours per week/Direct ABA, 1 hour per week/Parent Training

## CPT CODE FORMAT & SCHEDULE RULES

### CPT Codes Table
Format: CPT Code | Number of Hours Requested | Total Units | **Time Period** | Place of Service

Unit calculation: 1 hour = 4 units. Total Units = Hours per week × 26 weeks × 4. Show the calculation (e.g., "2,080 (20 hrs/wk × 26 wks × 4)").

**Time Period column:** For each code row, include checkboxes as follows and check the appropriate one:
☑ per week ☐ per month ☐ per auth
(Standard for all codes is "per week" unless otherwise specified in the notes.)

**CRITICAL — 97153 HOURS SOURCE RULE:**
The 97153 "Number of Hours Requested" MUST be taken from the BCBA's clinically RECOMMENDED hours — the medically necessary hours based on the assessment findings — NOT from the proposed schedule, family availability, or what the family agreed to. If the notes contain both a recommended amount and a proposed/agreed schedule that differs, always use the recommended/medically necessary figure in the CPT table. The Anticipated Schedule grid may reflect the family's proposed schedule, but the CPT table hours must reflect clinical recommendation.

**CRITICAL — 97155 HOURS CALCULATION RULE:**
The 97155 "Number of Hours Requested" MUST always be calculated as exactly 20% of the 97153 hours. Do NOT pull 97155 hours from the BCBA's notes, the proposed schedule, or any other source. Always compute: 97155 hours = 97153 hours × 0.20. Examples: if 97153 = 30 hrs/week, then 97155 = 6 hrs/week; if 97153 = 40 hrs/week, then 97155 = 8 hrs/week. This applies regardless of what the notes say about supervision hours.

Always include: 97151, 97153, 97155-GT, 97156-GT/U2.

### Anticipated Schedule
Generate the Anticipated Schedule as a **day-by-day grid** with a row for EVERY CPT code being requested. Format:

| CPT Code | Sunday | Monday | Tuesday | Wednesday | Thursday | Friday | Saturday |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 97153 | [hrs or —] | [hrs or —] | ... | ... | ... | ... | [hrs or —] |
| 97155 | [hrs or —] | [hrs or —] | ... | ... | ... | ... | [hrs or —] |
| 97156 | [hrs or —] | [hrs or —] | ... | ... | ... | ... | [hrs or —] |

Include a row for EVERY CPT code in the plan (97153, 97155, 97156 at minimum; add 97151, 97157 rows if applicable). Use "—" for days with no service. Ensure daily totals across all codes are consistent with the requested weekly hours. Reflect actual caregiver availability from the notes.

### Supervision Ratio Justification
Calculate the supervision ratio: 97155 hours ÷ 97153 hours. ALWAYS include a dedicated "Supervision Ratio Medical Necessity Justification" paragraph immediately before the CPT codes table, regardless of the percentage. This paragraph must:
- State the supervision ratio explicitly (e.g., "The supervision ratio for this client is X%")
- Cite the client's SPECIFIC challenging behaviors by topography, clinical complexity, skill deficit profile, and/or caregiver training needs that make this level of supervision medically necessary for THIS client
- Reference the client's severity level and any safety-critical behaviors (aggression, SIB, elopement, etc.) that require this frequency of BCBA oversight
- Explain why the requested supervision level is clinically appropriate for this client's presentation
- Explain why a lower supervision ratio would be clinically insufficient for this client

### Telehealth Split Documentation
When ANY CPT code uses the GT modifier (telehealth), you MUST include a "Telehealth Service Delivery Documentation" paragraph immediately following the CPT codes table. This paragraph must:
- Explicitly state the anticipated percentage split between in-person and telehealth for EACH code that uses the GT modifier
