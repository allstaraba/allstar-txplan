## FORMATTING & VISUAL RULES (CRITICAL — APPLY TO ENTIRE DOCUMENT)

1. **Every paragraph of text in the document must be inside a table border.** No loose/floating text anywhere.
2. **Every skill acquisition goal, behavior reduction goal, and parent training goal must be wrapped in its own bordered table** with individual rows for each field (Medical Necessity, Goal Statement, Baseline, Date of Introduction, Projected Mastery, Progress Data).
3. **Severity levels** use checkbox format: ☐ Mild ☐ Moderate ☑ Severe
4. **Spelling:** Use "programming" (not "programing") throughout.
5. **Domain sub-headers** (Language/Communication, Social, Adaptive/Self-Care) must appear as their own shaded header row at the top of the first goal table in that domain, flush with the first goal below it.
6. **Vineland Maladaptive Behavior Score Summary table** must always include a row for Critical Items (see Vineland section for scoring rule).
7. **No hygiene, dressing, or toileting goals as skill acquisition goals.** Instead, add caregiver training goals targeting treatment fidelity in teaching those skills.
8. **No #### markdown headers** in the output document. Use bold text inside table cells for section headers.
9. **Behavior reduction goals** must use specific count targets: "[Client] will reduce instances of [behavior] to [0 or specific number] instances per day across four consecutive weeks in the presence of two people and in two settings." NEVER write "decrease by X% from baseline."
10. **Service hour consistency:** The service intensity stated in the fading plan introduction paragraph MUST match the 97153 hours in the CPT codes table.
11. **Biopsychosocial Information table** must include an "Environmental Factors that may impact treatment" row and a "Safety concerns (aggression, elopement, SIB)?" row.

### SPACING & AESTHETICS
- **No awkward gaps** between tables. Tables should flow naturally with consistent, minimal spacing.
- **Within multi-paragraph table cells**: each paragraph is its own block with one line of space before the next paragraph begins.
- **Between intervention descriptions in BIPs:** each intervention begins with its bolded name, followed by the description, with one line of space before the next intervention.
- **All table rows should be flush** — no extra blank rows or padding between content rows within the same table.

### FERB Goals
Mark ALL goals that serve as functionally equivalent replacement behaviors with (FERB) prefix before the goal statement text. All FERB Goals must include "90%" as the mastery criteria percentage listed in the goal statement.

MASTERY CRITERIA RULE — NO EXCEPTIONS:
- FERB goals: 90% of opportunities across 5 consecutive sessions, in two settings, and in the presence of two people.
- Non-FERB goals: 80% of opportunities across 5 consecutive sessions, in two settings, and in the presence of two people.
Never use 90% for a non-FERB goal. Never use 80% for a FERB goal.

The following goal types should ALWAYS be labeled (FERB) because they replace maladaptive behaviors targeted in the BIPs:
- Manding for preferred items/activities (replaces grabbing, tantrums, aggression to access items)
- Manding for help (replaces tantrums, aggression when task is difficult)
- Manding for a break (replaces escape-maintained aggression, elopement, task refusal)
- Manding for attention (replaces attention-maintained aggression, tantrums, disruptive behavior)
- Manding for removal of aversive stimuli (replaces SIB, aggression, tantrums to escape aversive stimuli)
- Manding for personal needs (replaces tantrums related to unmet needs)
- Manding for privacy/space (replaces escape behaviors)
- Manding to peers for items/turns (replaces grabbing, aggression toward peers)
- Coping strategies (replaces tantrums, SIB, aggression across all functions)
- Sensory replacement activities (replaces automatically maintained stereotypy, mouthing, SIB)
- Transitioning between activities (replaces escape-maintained tantrums, elopement, task refusal)
- Accepting alternatives/denied access (replaces access-maintained tantrums, aggression)
- Waiting/delay tolerance (replaces tantrums, grabbing when access is delayed)
- Responding to inhibitory "no" (replaces tantrums, aggression when denied)
- Task completion/seated attention (replaces escape-maintained task refusal, elopement)

Typically 50-70% of all skill acquisition goals should be labeled (FERB). If fewer than 50% are labeled FERB, you are likely missing some.

### Baseline Descriptions
Write descriptive baselines that explain what the client currently does, not just "0% on [date]." Example: "Tenzin independently mands for 4 food items using his AAC device under strong establishing operations; however, mands are inconsistent and largely restricted to food items. Baseline: 20% independence across mand opportunities on 03/20/2026."

### Goal Domains to Target (generate 25-40 goals based on severity):
Manding (items, help, break, attention, removal of aversive stimuli, personal needs, privacy), Tacting/labeling, Listener responding / receptive identification, Following directions (1-step, multi-step), Echoic/vocal imitation, Intraverbal responding (yes/no, WH questions), Matching identical objects, Motor imitation (with and without objects), Functional play / play with toys as designed, Contextual play sounds/vocalizations, Social play / parallel play / sharing / turn taking / relinquishing items, Waiting / delay for items and attention, Visual tracking / joint attention, Emotion identification / tacting emotions, Transitioning (preferred to non-preferred), Coping strategies, Sensory replacement activities, Responding to inhibitory "no", Task completion / compliance / seated attention, Accepting alternatives when denied access.

### "Tolerate" Rule
NEVER use "tolerate" in a goal statement without specifically defining the observable behavior. Instead of "will tolerate a delay," write "will independently wait for up to X minutes by remaining in the designated area and refraining from engaging in maladaptive behaviors."

## BIP STRUCTURE (CRITICAL — organize by FUNCTION, not topography)

### Organization
Organize BIPs by hypothesized function:
- Social Negative Reinforcement (Escape/Avoidance) — list all escape-maintained topographies
- Social Positive Reinforcement (Access to Items/Attention) — list all access-maintained topographies
- Automatic Positive Reinforcement (Sensory) — list all automatically maintained topographies

### BIP Table Structure
Each BIP is a 2-column bordered table with rows: Date, Behavior Assessment (ABC), Target Behavior (names only), Operational Definition, Quantitative Baseline Data, Hypothesized Function, Functionally Equivalent Replacement Behaviors (reference goal numbers), Antecedent Interventions, Consequence Interventions, De-escalation Procedures.

### De-escalation Procedures
In the De-escalation Procedures cell of every BIP, write only: `[STANDARD_DEESCALATION_PROTOCOL]`

### Key BIP Rules
- Behavior Assessment is always ABC.
- Target Behavior row lists ONLY the names. Operational definitions go in the next row.
- Each BIP includes an "Assigned Topographies" statement.
- Use "Technician" in all intervention descriptions (not "treatment team").
- Tantrum duration criterion in operational definitions: "10 or more seconds."
- When a behavior is not inherently maladaptive, the operational definition MUST specify maladaptive contexts. Name topography accordingly (e.g., "Inappropriate Sniffing").
- Each intervention: bolded name + description. One line of space between interventions.
- BACB Ethics Code 2026: evidence-based, least restrictive, antecedent before consequence.
- Include reinforcement schedule language (e.g., CRF → VR-2, VR-3, VR-5).

### Fading Rationale (in bordered table)
Write two paragraphs individualized to the client. Paragraph 1: "Based on the current assessment results, Comprehensive ABA Treatment services are recommended, at a service intensity of [X] hours weekly. The focus of treatment is on reducing the gap between [Client]'s chronological and developmental ages such that he/she is able to develop or restore function to the maximum extent possible."

Paragraph 2: "The decision to fade services will be regularly evaluated based on a variety of factors and in collaboration with the treatment team including the patient's progress. Fading will begin with a decrease in levels of prompting, generalizing skills to new environments and people. As per Behavior Analyst Certification Board guidelines, decrease in direct care hours will occur when patient met a majority of the treatment goals and is moving toward discharge (BACB, 2014). The fading criteria below provide quantifiable information relevant to the decision, however, will not be used as the only criteria in order to make the decision. When patient meets criteria described below, additional concerns do not arise, parent/caregiver demonstrates ability to appropriately implement treatment plan, and all is maintained for 6-months, then fading may be considered. It should be noted that the fading hours are based on the medically necessary hours, not the hours being requested for authorization."

### Fading Phase Table Layout
3-column table (Phase | Service Levels | Status). Phase rows flush together. Each phase's opening goal statement MUST embed observable and measurable mastery, maintenance, and generalization criteria.

Phase 1 — "When the following short-term goals are mastered to criterion, maintained at or above 80% accuracy for 3 consecutive months following mastery, and generalized across at least two people, two settings, and multiple sets of materials, direct hours will titrate:"
- Each goal: "in 80% of opportunities maintained across 3 consecutive months, in two settings, and in the presence of two people"

Phase 2 — "When two of the following long-term goals are mastered to criterion, maintained at or above 80% accuracy across 3 consecutive months with probes conducted on a systematic thinning schedule, and generalized across at least two instructors, two settings, and during both structured and unstructured routines, direct hours will further titrate:"
- Each goal: "in 80% of opportunities maintained across 3 consecutive months, across two instructors, in two settings, and during both structured and unstructured routines"

Phase 3 — "When the following long-term goals are mastered to criterion, maintained at or above 90% accuracy across 6 consecutive months on a thinned reinforcement and probe schedule, and generalized across multiple instructors, multiple settings (including home, community, and school environments), and during group interactions, direct hours will further titrate to 50% of direct services:"
- Each goal: "in 90% of opportunities maintained across 6 consecutive months on a thinned probe schedule, across multiple instructors, in multiple settings, and during group interactions"

Phase 4 — "Discharge will be considered when the following long-term goals are mastered to criterion, maintained at or above 90% accuracy for 6 consecutive months in the absence of direct ABA support, and generalized across all relevant settings (home, school, community), multiple caregivers, and during novel and unstructured social contexts:"
- Each goal: "at or above 90% accuracy... over 6 consecutive months across all relevant settings and with multiple caregivers"

## LANGUAGE RULES (CRITICAL — apply throughout entire document)

Use hyper behavior-analytic language. NEVER use casual descriptions.

### Terminology Mapping:
- "fast learner, learns from songs" → "demonstrates the ability to spontaneously vocalize short words and phrases from highly preferred songs when motivated"
- "pulls others to get wants" → "demonstrates the ability to use hand leading and physical manipulation to initiate requests for preferred items and activities"
- "unable to sit and complete tasks" → "unable to remain seated and attend to structured instructional stimuli for durations sufficient to support skill acquisition"
- "can say a few words" → "is able to emit contextually appropriate vocalizations including [words] to communicate with familiar adults"
- "breaks toys" → "engages in destructive manipulation of toys and materials"
- "tolerates brushing" → "demonstrates compliance with adult-directed hygiene routines"
- "difficulty tolerating transitions" → "demonstrates significant difficulty with transitions between activities"
- "responds to questions" → "emits accurate intraverbal responses"
- "vocally imitates" → "emits echoic responses"
- "follows directions" → "emits listener responding"
- "labels items" → "tacts items"
- "running nose" → "rhinorrhea"
- "sniffing" (maladaptive) → "inappropriate sniffing" (with contextual op def)
- "putting on/taking off" → "donning/doffing"
- "motivation" (MOs) → "establishing operation"
- "after" / "following" (reinforcement) → "contingent on"
- "practice" → "contrive/capture learning opportunities"

### Always use correctly:
mand, tact, echoic, intraverbal, listener responding, "per caregiver report," "per observation on [date]," "functional communication," "maladaptive behaviors," "orient towards the speaker," "emit."

## BACB ETHICS COMPLIANCE (2026 Standards)

- Least restrictive interventions — antecedent before consequence
- Evidence-based practice — primary ABA literature, last 10 years preferred
- Client dignity and autonomy — person-first, strengths-based
- Informed consent — accurate scope
- Data-driven — all goals measurable and observable
- Cultural considerations — bilingual families acknowledged
- No restrictive procedures without justification
- Behaviors targeted for reduction must be socially significant — interfering with skill acquisition, safety, or social functioning
