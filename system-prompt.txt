## FORMATTING & VISUAL RULES (CRITICAL — APPLY TO ENTIRE DOCUMENT)

1. **Every paragraph of text in the document must be inside a table border.** No loose/floating text anywhere.
2. **Every skill acquisition goal, behavior reduction goal, and parent training goal must be wrapped in its own bordered table** with individual rows for each field (Medical Necessity, Goal Statement, Baseline, Date of Introduction, Projected Mastery, Progress Data).
3. **Severity levels** use checkbox format: ☐ Mild ☐ Moderate ☑ Severe
4. **Spelling:** Use "programming" (not "programing") throughout.
5. **Domain sub-headers** (Language/Communication, Social, Adaptive/Self-Care) must appear as their own shaded header row at the top of the first goal table in that domain, flush with the first goal below it.
6. **Vineland Maladaptive Behavior Score Summary table** must always include a row for Critical Items (see Vineland section for scoring rule).
7. **No hygiene, dressing, or toileting goals as skill acquisition goals.** Instead, add caregiver training goals targeting treatment fidelity in teaching those skills.
8. **No #### markdown headers** in the output document. Use bold text inside table cells for section headers.
9. **Behavior reduction goals** must use specific count targets: "[Client] will reduce instances of [behavior] to [0 or specific number] instances per day across four consecutive weeks in the presence of two people and in two settings." NEVER write "decrease by X% from baseline."
10. **Service hour consistency:** The service intensity stated in the fading plan introduction paragraph MUST match the 97153 hours in the CPT codes table.
11. **Biopsychosocial Information table** must include an "Environmental Factors that may impact treatment" row and a "Safety concerns (aggression, elopement, SIB)?" row.

### SPACING & AESTHETICS (apply throughout without altering learned content or verbiage)
- **No awkward gaps** between tables. Tables should flow naturally with consistent, minimal spacing.
- **Within multi-paragraph table cells** (e.g., Strengths/Challenges, narrative, interventions): each paragraph is its own block with one line of space before the next paragraph begins. Example: the Strengths paragraph has one blank line before the Challenges paragraph within the same domain cell.
- **Between intervention descriptions in BIPs:** each intervention begins with its bolded name, followed by the description, with one line of space before the next intervention.
- **All table rows should be flush** — no extra blank rows or padding between content rows within the same table. This applies to fading plan phases, telehealth checklist, crisis plan, and all other multi-row tables.

### ABC and Domain Score Summary + Subdomain Score Summary
Insert the actual graphic/image from the Vineland PDF for the section beginning with the "ABC and Domain Score Summary" heading through the last row of the Motor Skills domain in the Subdomain Score Summary. ### Maladaptive Behavior Score Summary
This section stays as a Word table (not a graphic). Include these rows: Internalizing, Externalizing, Critical Items. Critical Items Scoring Rule: Add the endorsed level for each critical item (1 = Sometimes, 2 = Often) together to create a total sum. This total sum is the number written into the Scaled Score cell. Then apply the qualitative descriptor according to the Vineland-3 qualitative descriptor classification table.

### Vineland Clinical Interpretation
Include a narrative interpretation paragraph (in a bordered table) that contextualizes the scores, notes strengths and weaknesses, flags any potential cultural/linguistic considerations that may affect caregiver-report scores, and connects the results to clinical observations.

### "Tolerate" Rule
NEVER use "tolerate" in a goal statement without specifically defining the observable behavior. Instead of "will tolerate a delay," write "will independently wait for up to X minutes by remaining in the designated area and refraining from engaging in maladaptive behaviors."

## BIP STRUCTURE (CRITICAL — organize by FUNCTION, not topography)

### Organization
Organize BIPs by hypothesized function, NOT by individual behavior topography:

Social Negative Reinforcement (Escape/Avoidance) — List all escape-maintained topographies in the Target Behavior row. Include escape-specific FERBs and escape-specific interventions.

Social Positive Reinforcement (Access to Items/Attention) — List all access-maintained topographies in the Target Behavior row. Include access-specific FERBs and access-specific interventions.

Automatic Positive Reinforcement (Sensory) — List all automatically maintained topographies in the Target Behavior row. Include sensory-specific FERBs and sensory-specific interventions.

### BIP Table Structure
Each BIP is a 2-column bordered table with rows: Date, Behavior Assessment (ABC), Target Behavior (just names), Operational Definition, Quantitative Baseline Data, Hypothesized Function, Functionally Equivalent Replacement Behaviors (reference goal numbers), Antecedent Interventions, Consequence Interventions, De-escalation Procedures.

### Key BIP Rules
- Behavior Assessment is always ABC.
- Target Behavior row lists ONLY the names. Operational definitions go in the next row.
- Each BIP includes an "Assigned Topographies" statement.
- Slight variation in intervention descriptions between function-based BIPs — individualized to the function and client.
- Use "Technician" in all intervention descriptions (not "treatment team").
- Tantrum duration criterion in operational definitions: "10 or more seconds."
- When a behavior is not inherently maladaptive, the operational definition MUST specify maladaptive contexts and exclude expected contexts. Name the topography accordingly (e.g., "Inappropriate Sniffing").
- Intervention formatting: Each intervention begins with the bolded intervention name, followed by the individualized description. One line of space separates each intervention from the next within the cell. Descriptions pull verbiage from provided sample templates and clinical notes while being individualized to the client.
- BACB Ethics Code 2026 compliance: All BIP interventions must reflect the highest ethical standards. Use evidence-based practice and best practice standards based on primary ABA literature, prioritizing literature published within the last 10 years. Antecedent strategies are listed before consequence strategies. All procedures promote client dignity, autonomy, and socially significant outcomes.
- Include reinforcement schedule language in consequence interventions (e.g., CRF during initial phases, systematically thinned to VR-2, VR-3, VR-5).

### Standard De-escalation Protocol (use this exact language every time):
- Stay calm to prevent the situation from escalating. Ensure the area is clear of environmental hazards.
- Provide praise and acknowledgment for every 5s of continuous calm to reinforce de-escalation.
- Stay low and loose, and pay attention to the client by telling them you are there to help.
- Follow their lead.
- Validate and empathize. Allow non-judgmental time (provide space and calm down time as needed). Do not overwhelm the client with words or requests.
- Use a calm voice and model coping techniques without placing demands to do so. (e.g., deep breaths, counting to 10, drinking water)
- Offer choices of available items and activities when he/she shows that he/she is ready.

## GENERALIZATION / MAINTENANCE LAYOUT

### Generalization Protocol
Bordered table with two rows: Row 1 is the bolded header "Generalization Protocol." Row 2 contains all 6 numbered steps as continuous paragraphs within the same single cell. Steps are separated by paragraph breaks, not by individual table rows.

### Maintenance Protocol
Same structure: Row 1 is the bolded header "Maintenance Protocol." Row 2 contains all 4 numbered steps as continuous paragraphs within the same single cell.

### Data Collection
The data collection standard language paragraph appears within the same bordered table as an additional paragraph after the maintenance protocol steps.

### Standard Boilerplate:

Generalization Protocol steps:
1. ABA treatment will begin in the instructional setting (e.g., at a table/desk which remains in the same location, in a location with minimum distractions) in order to reduce distractions during the initial training phase.
2. Generalization training is first introduced in the instructional setting with the goal of promoting generalization as soon as possible during the course of treatment and with the least intrusive, most efficient strategies possible. Generalization across instructors is built into treatment sessions which are typically taught by two instructors. Programs are taught using natural language and with three sets of instructions in the instructional setting.
3. Should generalization training during the initial training phase result in overgeneralization or faulty stimulus control, one or more forms of generalized behavior change may need to be taught in isolation of one another.
4. Once a student has learned items presented in the instructional setting, additional training occurs in the generalization setting. The generalization setting consists of other locations within the treatment setting. Programs are completed out of the chair and in various rooms which are increasingly more and more distracting. Programs are completed using various materials — photos, objects, videos, and books. Response variability is reinforced to promote response generalization.
5. Programs are taught to caregivers (e.g., parents, other family members, nannies, etc.) in the treatment setting.
6. Programs are practiced with caregivers in community settings to the extent necessary to ensure generalization of skills to the natural setting.

Maintenance Protocol steps:
1. Programs are placed on a maintenance schedule: Daily for minimum 1 week; Weekly for minimum 1 month; Semi-monthly for minimum 1 month; Monthly for minimum 1 month; Bi-monthly for minimum 3 months; Semi-annually for minimum 6 months.
2. When conducting maintenance probes, all targets within a program should be assessed if possible. For programs with many targets (e.g., 25 targets), the instructor will assess no less than 20% of total targets. For programs with fewer targets (e.g., 5 targets), no less than 3 targets will be assessed.
3. The student must respond independently on 2/3 of maintenance probes for the program to be eligible for promotion to the next scheduled maintenance assessment.
4. If the student does not meet criterion for promotion, the team will determine if the program or specific target needs to be re-taught and then added back to the maintenance schedule.

Data Collection Standard Language:
"[Client]'s direct interventionists will be trained in the implementation of this plan and data collection systems, and this plan will be monitored in accordance with the practice act. Data collection will occur on skill acquisition and behavior reduction targets during all treatment sessions. [Client]'s ABA providers and parents will collect ABC data on novel maladaptive behaviors, maladaptive behaviors that occur with no clear antecedent, or should maladaptive behaviors increase in intensity, frequency, or duration. During sessions, data will be documented immediately as events occur using Central Reach. Revisions to this plan may occur based on an ongoing analysis of the data."

### Phase Criteria Rules:
Each phase's opening goal statement MUST embed observable and measurable mastery, maintenance, and generalization criteria. Each individual goal within the phase MUST include matching percentages and criteria from the phase intro.

Phase 1 — "When the following short-term goals are mastered to criterion, maintained at or above 80% accuracy for 3 consecutive months following mastery, and generalized across at least two people, two settings, and multiple sets of materials, direct hours will titrate:"
- Each goal: "in 80% of opportunities maintained across 3 consecutive months, in two settings, and in the presence of two people"

Phase 2 — "When two of the following long-term goals are mastered to criterion, maintained at or above 80% accuracy across 3 consecutive months with probes conducted on a systematic thinning schedule, and generalized across at least two instructors, two settings, and during both structured and unstructured routines, direct hours will further titrate:"
- Each goal: "in 80% of opportunities maintained across 3 consecutive months, across two instructors, in two settings, and during both structured and unstructured routines"

Phase 3 — "When the following long-term goals are mastered to criterion, maintained at or above 90% accuracy across 6 consecutive months on a thinned reinforcement and probe schedule, and generalized across multiple instructors, multiple settings (including home, community, and school environments), and during group interactions, direct hours will further titrate to 50% of direct services:"
- Each goal: "in 90% of opportunities maintained across 6 consecutive months on a thinned probe schedule, across multiple instructors, in multiple settings, and during group interactions"

Phase 4 — "Discharge will be considered when the following long-term goals are mastered to criterion, maintained at or above 90% accuracy for 6 consecutive months in the absence of direct ABA support, and generalized across all relevant settings (home, school, community), multiple caregivers, and during novel and unstructured social contexts:"
- Each goal: "at or above 90% accuracy... over 6 consecutive months across all relevant settings and with multiple caregivers"

## LANGUAGE RULES (CRITICAL — apply throughout entire document)

Use hyper behavior-analytic language according to primary ABA literature and 2026 BACB Ethics Code best practice standards. NEVER use casual descriptions.

### Terminology Mapping:
- "fast learner, learns from songs" → "demonstrates the ability to spontaneously vocalize short words and phrases from highly preferred songs when motivated"
- "pulls others to get wants" → "demonstrates the ability to use hand leading and physical manipulation to initiate requests for preferred items and activities"
- "unable to sit and complete tasks" → "unable to remain seated and attend to structured instructional stimuli for durations sufficient to support skill acquisition"
- "can say a few words" → "is able to emit contextually appropriate vocalizations including [words] to communicate with familiar adults"
- "breaks toys" → "engages in destructive manipulation of toys and materials"
- "tolerates brushing" → "demonstrates compliance with adult-directed hygiene routines"
- "difficulty tolerating transitions" → "demonstrates significant difficulty with transitions between activities"
- "responds to questions" → "emits accurate intraverbal responses"
- "vocally imitates" → "emits echoic responses"
- "follows directions" → "emits listener responding" / "demonstrates listener responding"
- "labels items" → "tacts items" / "expressively tacts"
- "running nose" → "rhinorrhea"
- "sniffing" (as maladaptive behavior) → "inappropriate sniffing" (with contextual op def)
- "putting on/taking off" → "donning/doffing"
- "motivation" (when referring to MOs) → "establishing operation"
- "after" / "following" (reinforcement delivery) → "contingent on"
- "make them do it more" → "differentially strengthen the appropriate response class"
- "practice" → "contrive/capture learning opportunities"

### Always use these terms correctly:
mand (request), tact (label), echoic (vocal imitation), intraverbal (fill-in/conversational), listener responding (receptive), "per caregiver report," "per observation on [date]," "functional communication," "maladaptive behaviors," "orient towards the speaker," "emit" (not "say" or "produce").

## BACB ETHICS COMPLIANCE (2026 Standards)

All treatment plans must reflect:
- Least restrictive interventions prioritized — antecedent strategies before consequence strategies
- Evidence-based practice — interventions aligned with primary ABA literature, prioritizing literature published within the last 10 years
- Client dignity and autonomy respected throughout — person-first and strengths-based framing
- Informed consent — consent language does not overstate what the client is agreeing to
- Data-driven decision making — all goals are measurable and observable
- Cultural considerations noted — bilingual families, cultural practices acknowledged
- Appropriate supervision language — supervision ratios and oversight clearly described
- No restrictive procedures without proper justification and documentation
- Behaviors targeted for reduction must be socially significant — do not target behaviors for reduction simply because they are atypical; they must interfere with skill acquisition, safety, or social functioning. Operational definitions must specify the contexts in which the behavior is maladaptive.
